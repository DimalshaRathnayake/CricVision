Research Domain

Domain, gap, problem, objectives and methodology

Automatic analysis of cricket videos through multimodal AI, event detection, and player-based retrieval.

Literature Survey

Sports video analysis has moved from rule-based event detection to deep learning and multimodal systems. In cricket, existing methods often detect boundaries, wickets, or highlights, but many still need manual processing or focus on short clips instead of full-length, player-specific retrieval.

OCR and STT techniques can extract contextual signals from scoreboards and commentary, while computer vision models can detect visual cues such as umpire gestures. Semantic embeddings and vector search make it possible to retrieve match moments quickly through natural language queries.

Research Gap

Existing systems do not sufficiently support full-match player-based filtering, automatic navigation to player-specific moments, or reliable player-event linking with timestamps. Near-miss wicket events and batsman/bowler change tracking are also rarely handled in traditional highlight-generation systems.

Research Problem

Long cricket match videos require users to manually scroll through hours of footage to find specific player actions and events such as FOUR, SIX, WICKET, and wicket-related near misses. This wastes time for fans, coaches, analysts, broadcasters, and training institutions.

Research Objectives

  • Segment cricket videos based on player identity.
  • Detect and index important cricket events with timestamps.
  • Link players with detected key events.
  • Support player-wise filtering and event-wise navigation.
  • Enable semantic search for fast match review.
Methodology

Multimodal cricket video intelligence pipeline

The system turns unstructured match videos into searchable event timelines.

01

Video Preprocessing

Frames are sampled from cricket videos and relevant areas such as scoreboards and umpire regions are extracted.

02

Scoreboard OCR

EasyOCR extracts scoreboard text. Cleaning, token filtering, and player-name matching improve reliability.

03

Event Detection

EfficientNet-B0 detects umpire frames and ResNet-18 classifies event gestures such as FOUR, SIX, WICKET, and NO ACTION.

04

Player Identification

NER and roster matching identify batsmen and bowlers from text streams and map them to detected events.

05

Timeline Construction

Events and player identities are synchronized using timestamps to create structured video segments.

06

Semantic Search

MiniLM embeddings and FAISS-style vector retrieval enable natural-language search over match moments.
